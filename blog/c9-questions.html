Asking the Right Questions
During learning computer programming, there are many questions coming up. Sometimes I can solve a problem on my own, however when the problem is either too difficult to perceive or too time-consuming, it’s a good time to ask for help. To get the most helpful answer, we need to ask the right question.

Giving the Context

Although experts will quickly find out the context, it’s a good practice to give context in the question. It helps everyone to quickly understand the situation and save time.

ExamplesHelp a Lot

A problem is a problem. Sometimes a problem may be more difficult to describe in word. Some people like to post their question without providing any examples. It’s always a good thing to provide actual examples where the problem occurs so others can analyze. Without an example, others may find examples from their own experience with a lot of assumptions. The answer may not solve the particular problem.
